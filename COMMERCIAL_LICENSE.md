# Commercial Licensing

Korpus is available under two licensing paths:

- **AGPLv3** for open-source and AGPL-compatible use
- **Commercial license** for organizations that want to use Korpus without AGPL obligations

## When you need a commercial license

You likely need a commercial license if you want to:

- use Korpus in a closed-source or proprietary product
- offer Korpus as part of a paid hosted service
- modify Korpus without releasing those modifications under AGPLv3
- embed Korpus into a commercial internal platform and avoid AGPL compliance risk

## What the commercial license gives you

A commercial license can provide terms such as:

- use in proprietary systems
- closed-source modifications
- commercial distribution rights
- negotiated support or maintenance terms

## How to obtain one

To discuss commercial licensing, contact the repository owner.

You can include:

- your company or project name
- intended use case
- deployment model
- expected scale
- whether you need support or custom terms
